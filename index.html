Interesting Facts About Camel

  1. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  2. Chameleons vary wildly in term of size.
  3. Chameleons mainly change colour in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

  1. Piegons can recognise each letter of human albhabet
  2. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  3. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Interesting Facts About Teddy bear

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. The largest collection of teddies ever auctioned belonged to American multi-million dollar investment scammer Paul Greenwood. His collection of 1,300 bears was auctioned at Christie’s for £1.1million.
  3. A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  1. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  2. Pandas need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation.
  3. Sometimes, to mark their scent, panda's climb a tree backwards with their hindfeet until they're in a full handstand upside down - enabling them to leave their scent higher up.

Interesting Facts About Flying cicada

  1. Cicadas can live a relatively long time: 4 to 17 years, depending on whether they are annual or periodical cicadas. The periodical cicadas live the longest.
  2. Cicadas are herbivores. This means they eat vegetation. Young cicadas eat liquid from plant roots, while molting cicadas eat twigs.
  3. The buzzing sound they make is a mating call. Male cicadas vibrate a white, drumlike membrane on their abdomens called a tymbal.

Interesting Facts About Frog

  1. A frog’s eyes and nose are on the very top of their heads.
  2. The world’s largest frog species is known as the ‘Goliath Frog’.
  3. Frogs drink water through their skin.

Interesting Facts About Dog

  1. The Beatles song ‘A Day in the Life’ has a frequency only dogs can hear.
  2. A Bloodhound’s sense of smell can be used as evidence in court.
  3. A Greyhound could beat a Cheetah in a long distance race.
